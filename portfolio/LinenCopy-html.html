Linen is possibly one of the oldest fibers used in textile history, with

dyed linen fabric remnants being found in caves in West Asia dating

as far back as 36,000 BCE. Linen is 100% natural plant based fiber

that has a long storied use throughout the world.

Starting as a simple flax plant, the reeds are then soak in a process

called retting, then further broken down via scutching, and then

“heckled” to removed the last reedy bits from the strong long fibers

before it is dyed and woven into fabric. Because this process is both

labour and resource intensive, reusing already manufactured fabric

is an eco-friendly alternative.

Because linen is such a strong and sturdy natural fiber, garments

made with linen will create a long lasting and sustainable garment.

And because linen is simply just plants, washing and wearing linen

won’t shed micro-plastics into the environment, making linen such

an exciting and sustainable fashion statement!
